Does China's Past Population Give Us an Insight Into Their Future?

Visualizations created by Michael Rooney

Chinese population data exploration

The Chinese Population has made its way to 1.4 billion in the year 2024. However, many experts are saying that China is in trouble due to their so called demographic bomb. In fact, since 2022 China's population has been decreasing after a steady period of consistent growth. The shrinking population issue is worsened by the fact that the population is aging. These problems primarily stem from the one-child policy implemented in 1980 and different administrative divisions looking to adhere to the birth rates put out by the central government of the People's Republic of China. Other events which may have had an effect on both population and birth rates is China's Great Famine beginning in 1959 killing nearly 30 million Chinese citizens. Lastly, Chinese women are contributing to this demographic crisis as well because none of them want to leave their careers or pay the high child care costs. Unfortunately, in a country with very little history of paternity leave, those are the woman's two options.
